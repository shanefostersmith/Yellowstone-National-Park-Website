Directions to Yellowstone

Airports

Yellowstone is accessible via several airports: Bozeman Yellowstone International Airport in Montana, and Jackson Hole Airport in Wyoming are the closest. Both airports offer car rental services.

Roads

Yellowstone has five entrance stations –the North Entrance, Northeast Entrance, East Entrance, South Entrance, and West Entrance– and several are closed to regular vehicles during winter. It takes many hours to drive between these entrances, so be sure to check the status of roads at the park's website when planning your trip.

Transportation

The primary way visitors get to Yellowstone is by car. However, there is a bus service from Bozeman to West Yellowstone. There is also commercial transportation from Bozeman to Gardiner during the winter and summer seasons and commercial transportation from Cody and Jackson in the summer. Furthermore, some bus tours will be able to bring you around the park if you book them.

Be sure to take a look at the offical National Park Service map to get idea of where the main entrances, roads and information centers are located.

Lodging

There are a multitude of lodging options to choose from at Yellowstone National Park. Make sure to make your reservations as early as possible, especially during the peak months of June, July and August. You can make your reservations at this site.

Here are some of the most popular lodgings avaliable

  • Canyon Lodge and Cabins: These rooms are only avaliable during the summer season and are located near the Canyon Village. The main building includes a restaurant.
  • Grant Village Lodge: These rooms are only avaliable during the summer season and are located near the West Thumb of Yellowstone Lake. This lodging complex includes a restaurant.
  • Lake Hotel and Cabins: These rooms are only avaliable during the summer seaso and are located near the Yellowstone Lake. This lodging includes a restaurant, bar, deli and business center.
  • Lake Lodge Cabins: These rooms are only avaliable during the summer season and are located near the Yellowstone Lake. This lodging does not include a restaurant, but does include a comfortable lobby with fireplaces.
  • Mammoth Hot Springs Hotel and Cabins: These rooms are avaliable during the summer and winter seasons and are located near the Mammoth Hot Springs. You can find equipment, lessons and guided tours at this location.
  • Old Faithful Inn: These rooms are only avalible during the summer season and are perhaps the most popular rooms in the entire park. This lodging is located near the Old Faithful Visitor Center.
  • Old Faithful Lodge: These rooms are only avaliable during the summer season and are located near the Old Faithful East Parking lot. The lobby has a great view of Old Faithful and includes a food-court.
  • Old Faithful Snow Lodge: These rooms are avaliable during the summer and winter season. This lodging is located near the Old Faithful Visitor Center and includes a restaurant, winter equipment, tours and shuttles (during the winter).
  • Roosevelt Lodge: These rooms are only avaliable in the summer season and are located near the Tower Ranger Station. This lodging offers horseback and stagecoach rides.

Passes and Permits

In order to enter Yellowstone National Park, you must purchase a pass. The money from these passes is critical for maintaining the park's services. You can purchase these passes online at this website or at any of the entrance stations.

>

Here are the types of entrance passes you can purchase

  • Seven-Entrance Passes
    • Single Vechicle: $35
    • Bus/Van: $20 per person
    • Single Motorcycle/Snowmobile: $30
    • Single Motorcycle/Snowmobile: $30
    • Single Person (no vechicle): $20
  • Annual Passes: $70 (and $20 for seniors over the age of 62)
  • Lifetime Passes: These are only avaliable for seniors ($80) and free for those with permanent disability or a veteran
>

Camping Fees

To be able to camp at one of the many campgrounds at Yellowstone, you must purchase a pass. You can find the full list of campgrounds, their accessability and their associated fees at the National Park Service's campgrounds page

Fishing permits

In order to fish at Yellowstone, you must buy a permit (at this website) and only fish at approved areas

Here are the types of permits avaliable:


  • Season-Long Pass: $75
  • 7-Day Pass: $55
  • 3-Day Pass: $40

Boating Permits

In order to boat at Yellowstone, you must buy a permit and get your boat inspected at one of the approved locations.

Here are the types of permits avaliable:

  • Non-Motorized Boat: $20 for 7 days or $30 for the entire season
  • Motorized Boat: $40 for 7 days or $60 for the entire season

Here are the locations where you can get your boat inspected:

  • Grant Village Backcountry Office
  • Bridge Bay Ranger Station
  • Lewis Lake Ranger Station

Other Considerations

Before you go to Yellowstone, be sure to check out the alerts avaliable at the National Park Service's Yellowstone National Park website. Depending on the season, mantainence or other events, certain roads and sites may be closed, so be sure that you are able to visit what you want to visit. It is also recommended that you review all of the park's rules and regulations before visiting in order to be safe. Furthermore, it is important that you check if there are any dangerous weather conditions and plan accordingly. With just a little planning ahead, you can make sure your trip to Yellowstone National park is as magical as it should be!
