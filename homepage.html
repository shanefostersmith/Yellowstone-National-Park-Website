YELLOWSTONE NATIONAL PARK

"Yellowstone, of all the national parks, is the wildest and most universal in its appeal... Daily new, always strange, ever full of change, it is Nature's wonder park. It is the most human and the most popular of all parks."

- Dr. Susan S. Rugh, Professor of History at Brigham Young University

Welcome to Yellowstone National Park, the world's first national park and a testament to the preservation of untouched, natural beauty. Established in 1872, this sprawling expanse of wilderness, which stretches over an impressive 2.2 million acres, is a crown jewel of America's national parks, nestled in the heart of the West. Each year, millions of visitors from across the globe are drawn to Yellowstone, captivated by its breathtaking landscapes, remarkable geothermal features, abundant wildlife, and boundless opportunities for adventure and exploration.

Yellowstone is a geologist's dream, boasting one of the world's largest active volcanic systems. It is home to the famous Old Faithful geyser, which, true to its name, reliably erupts to the delight of onlookers. The park also houses the stunning Grand Prismatic Spring, the largest hot spring in the United States, known for its brilliant, rainbow-like colors. The Grand Canyon of Yellowstone, a dramatic and picturesque canyon carved by the Yellowstone River, offers awe-inspiring vistas and spectacular hiking opportunities.

In addition to its geological wonders, Yellowstone is a thriving ecosystem that supports a rich diversity of plants and animals. From lush forests and expansive meadows, to tranquil lakes and meandering rivers, these varied habitats provide homes for an array of wildlife. Visitors may spot herds of bison grazing in the Lamar Valley, catch sight of a solitary grizzly bear or wolf, or witness a bald eagle soaring overhead. Yellowstone is a haven for birdwatchers, with over 300 bird species calling the park home.

The park's vastness invites outdoor enthusiasts to indulge in a myriad of recreational activities. Whether it's hiking through pristine wilderness, fishing in world-renowned rivers, or camping under the stars, there's something for everyone in Yellowstone. For those seeking a more relaxed experience, guided tours offer a wealth of knowledge about the park's history, geology, and wildlife, all while navigating Yellowstone's extensive sights.

Boasting a rich cultural history, Yellowstone is also a place to uncover the past. From early Native American tribes to pioneering explorers, the park has long been a place of significance. Visitors can explore various historical sites and museums within the park to delve deeper into its storied past.

So whether you're an adventurer at heart, a lover of wildlife, a geology enthusiast, or someone seeking solace in nature's grandeur, Yellowstone National Park invites you to embark on an unforgettable journey into the wild. Come, explore, and be inspired by the magic and wonder of this unparalleled natural sanctuary.
